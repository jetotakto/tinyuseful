How to calculate a tip manually

  1. For 10%: move the decimal point one place left ($45.00 → $4.50).
  2. For 20%: take 10% and double it ($4.50 × 2 = $9.00).
  3. For 15% or 18%: take 10%, then add half (15%) or close to half plus a bit (18%).

Example: 18% tip on a $45 bill = $4.50 + $4.50 + $0.90 ≈ $8.10.

Quick examples

  • 15% tip on $45 = $6.75
  • 18% tip on $50 = $9.00
  • 20% tip on $60 = $12.00
  • 22% tip on $80 = $17.60
  • $90 bill + 20% split between 3 = $36.00 each

Formulas used

tip = bill × (percent ÷ 100)

total = bill + tip

per person = total ÷ split

In the US, standard restaurant tip is 18–20% on the pre-tax subtotal. 15% is acceptable for adequate service, 20%+ for great service. In the UK and most of continental Europe, service is often included; 5–10% extra is appreciated but not expected. In Japan, tipping is usually not done at all.

Common uses

  • Restaurant and café service in the US (sit-down, 18–22%)
  • Splitting a shared dinner bill between people
  • Bartenders and baristas (typically $1–$2 or 15–20%)
  • Hotel housekeeping and bellhop service
  • Hairdresser, taxi, and food delivery (10–20%)

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate a 15% or 18% tip in my head?

For 15%: take 10% (move the decimal one place left), then add half of that. Bill of $40 → 10% is $4 → half is $2 → 15% is $6. For 18%: same trick, then add a bit more. For 20%: take 10% and double it.

Should I tip on the pre-tax or post-tax total?

Pre-tax is the traditional standard in the US — you're tipping for service, not for tax. But many people tip on the post-tax total because it's what's printed on the receipt and it's easier. Both are acceptable.

Is 20% standard in 2026?

In US sit-down restaurants, yes. "Tip creep" has pushed the expected range from 15–18% (2010s) to 18–22% (2020s). Counter-service and takeout are more flexible — 10% or a few dollars is common, sometimes nothing.

How do I split a bill unevenly?

This calculator does an even split. For uneven splits, ask the server to print separate checks (most US restaurants will), or calculate each person's share manually and add a flat tip percentage to each.
