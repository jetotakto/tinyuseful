How to calculate a discount manually

  1. Convert the discount percentage to a decimal (25% → 0.25).
  2. Multiply the price by the decimal to get the savings amount.
  3. Subtract savings from price for the final price.

Example: 25% off $80 → 80 × 0.25 = $20 savings → final price $60.

Quick examples

  • 20% off $50 = $40 (saves $10)
  • 30% off $120 = $84 (saves $36)
  • 50% off $200 = $100 (saves $100)
  • "50% off then 20% off" $100 = $40 (60% total, NOT 70%)
  • Buy one get one 50% off (2 × $40) = $60 (25% effective discount)

Formulas used

savings = price × (discount ÷ 100)

final price = price − savings

final price = price × (1 − discount ÷ 100) (equivalent)

Two discounts in sequence don't add up. 50% off then 20% off is not 70% off — it's 60% off (because the 20% comes off the already-reduced price). For coupon stacking and Black Friday markdowns, run each discount one at a time.

Common uses

  • X percent off calculator — quick price-off check before checkout
  • Black Friday and seasonal sale comparisons
  • Stacking a coupon on top of a sale price
  • Computing membership/loyalty discount on a final cart
  • Verifying a "X% off" sticker against the receipt
  • Negotiating discounts with a supplier or contractor

Frequently asked questions

If something is "50% off" and I have a 20% coupon, what's the final discount?

60%, not 70%. The 20% comes off the already-discounted price. $100 → 50% off → $50 → 20% off → $40. Final price is 40% of original = 60% discount total.

How do I find the original price from a sale price?

Original = final ÷ (1 − discount/100). If you paid $60 with 25% off, original was $60 ÷ 0.75 = $80. Useful for verifying the discount actually matches what's claimed.

Is "buy one get one free" the same as 50% off?

Yes, mathematically — assuming you wanted both items. If you only wanted one, BOGO is 0% off (you're paying full price for the one you wanted). 50% off is straightforwardly cheaper for any quantity.

Do these calculations include sales tax?

No. Discount is applied to the pre-tax price; tax is then added to the discounted total. Use the Sales Tax Calculator for the next step.
